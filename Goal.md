### The Goal 

The primary goal for Backend4Dummies is to be an entry point into Backend Software Development. 

It's been my experience that the first step to learning is **inituition**. As such, my focus is on writing short descritpons of very complex concepts to get gist. The  

Further, I'd like to suggest that we can think of all 'educational  media' in three diffferent ways. The first are intution builders. The second are highly detailed summaries, and the thrid are quick cheat-sheets. Currently Backend4Dummies is primarly geared for the last 2 categories. 

The first reason for this pedagodical approach is practical.In order to write a detialed explanation of a given project,I have to be an expert. I'd require a an inordinate intellectual hubris to even attempt this feat. However, it would be pretty cool. 

The second reason for this strategy lies in part of the reason I fell inlove with programming. To build something substanstial (presumably why one would read something like this), ***there's no need to know everything!*** Abstraction enables a faith in software you don't use. Open-Source, Well Trusted and Extensive Libraries allow us to blindly use tools made by other people, without building everything from scratch.


In a lot of ways, the elaboring word form before is complete relevant to the central point. Put simply, the concept of abstracttion (in applied in a general context) is to trust others. It's epistemic humility to understand that you comprehend every part of a system, and to think otherwise is quite absurd. 

In more ways than one, the building of software is a communal  project, its the compiliation of developers sitting in rooms accorss the globe, pushing code to make internet work. It's a historical archive, relaying the needs, understandings, of the world.
